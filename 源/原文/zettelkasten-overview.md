# Zettelkasten Overview — zettelkasten.de 官方全貌

## The Introduction to the Zettelkasten Method

Are you new to the Method? Read the Introduction to the Zettelkasten Method and learn what makes the Zettelkasten work as a system, how to create notes, how to connect notes to make a web of knowledge, and how to create structures so you stay in control of the growing knowledge web.

Using a Zettelkasten is about optimizing your workflow of learning and producing knowledge. The products are texts, mostly.

## Common Questions

- How to Start a Zettelkasten When You Are Stuck in Theory
- **How many Zettelkästen should I have?** The answer is, most likely, only one for the duration of your life. But there are exceptions to this rule.
- **Don't use categories. Use tags instead.**
- "What should my first note be?" — it doesn't matter. Just get started.
- **Set links between notes.** Full-text search on its own provides not enough information. Connections will do, especially in the long run.
- You have to interpret your sources and then rely on your own thoughts henceforth to get the maximum benefit.
- **Collecting information does not increase your knowledge.**

## Principles

- **Principle of Atomicity**: put things that belong together into a single note, give it an ID, but limit its content to that single topic.
- **Principle of Connectivity**: links vs note sequences. Different Kinds of Ties Between Notes.

## Knowledge Management

- Building blocks: **inbox**, **note archive**, **reference manager**
- Use a real notebook to capture thoughts
- Write notes in a way you understand later
- Add Identity to things to make references possible
- Don't use categories. Use tags instead.
- Set links between notes. Search alone is not enough.
- Every Zettel has two forms: an inner and outer form.
- **The Collector's Fallacy**: you have to work with new material to really learn it.

## Scaling

- Use temporary "buffer" notes to collect stuff that you can later re-arrange.
- **Layers of Evidence**: Data description → Interpretation → Synthesis
- **Structural Layers**:
  - Bottom: Content
  - Middle: Structure Notes
  - Top: Main Structure Notes and Double Hashes (special tags)

## Writing & Reading

- Make writing a part of your identity
- Divide composing from editing
- Publish iteratively
- The Barbell Method of Reading: read thoroughly when necessary, skim for the rest
