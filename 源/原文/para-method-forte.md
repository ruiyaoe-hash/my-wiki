# The PARA Method — Tiago Forte 原文

## Core Claim

The ideal organizational system is one that leads directly to tangible benefits. It should be **actionable** — instead of putting obstacles in your way, it pulls actions closer and makes them easier to start and finish.

## Four Categories

1. **Projects** — short-term efforts with a certain goal. Examples: Complete webpage design, Buy a new computer, Write research report.
2. **Areas of responsibility** — ongoing attention required. Examples: Health, Finances, Kids, Writing, Car, Home. Work examples: Marketing, HR, Product Management, Engineering.
3. **Resources** — topics you're interested in and learning about. Examples: Graphic design, Organic gardening, Coffee, Modern architecture, Notetaking, Breathwork, Photography.
4. **Archives** — inactive items from the above three categories. Completed projects, inactive areas, resources no longer interested in.

## Key Principle

> We were taught to categorize by academic subject, such as Math, History, or Chemistry. But then without realizing it, we took that same approach into adulthood.

Organize by **projects and goals**, NOT by academic subject.

## The Simplicity Imperative

> If your organizational system is as complex as your life, then the demands of maintaining it will end up robbing you of the time and energy you need to live that life.

The system has to be so simple that it frees up your attention, instead of taking more of it. Your system has to give you time, not take time.
